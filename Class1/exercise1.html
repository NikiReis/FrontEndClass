Silicon Valley Bank Failure

The failure and the world consequences

After turning on CNBC last Thursday to see SVBs stock price getting hammered and news of venture firms urging startups to hit the exits,

EarthOptics CEO Lars Dyrud acted quickly. At 4 p.m. ET, he requested a $25 million wire transfer from Silicon Valley Bank, representing roughly 90% of his companys deposits

It was too late. EarthOptics didnt get a response on Thursday, and the following day SVB was seized by regulators in the second-largest bank failure in U.S. history.

Dyrud had no idea when hed be able to access his companys deposits, as the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. only guarantees $250,000 per client.
